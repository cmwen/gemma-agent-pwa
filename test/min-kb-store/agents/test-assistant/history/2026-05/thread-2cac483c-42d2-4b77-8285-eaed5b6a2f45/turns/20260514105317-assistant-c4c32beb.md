The sky appears blue due to a phenomenon called Rayleigh scattering. Sunlight is composed of different wavelengths (colors). When sunlight enters Earth's atmosphere, it scaters off the gas molecules and tiny particles in the air. Shorter wavelengths, like those from blue and violet light, are scattered more effectively than longer wavelengths, like red and orange light. Because blue light is scattered across the entire sky, we perceive the sky as blue.
